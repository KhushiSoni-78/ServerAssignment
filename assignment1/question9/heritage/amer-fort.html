Overview

Amer Fort is one of the most iconic landmarks of Jaipur and a brilliant example of Rajput architecture. Located on a hilltop overlooking Maota Lake, the fort attracts millions of tourists every year.

History

Amer Fort was constructed in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I and later expanded by successive rulers. It served as the royal residence of the Kachwaha dynasty before Jaipur city was founded.

Built primarily using red sandstone and marble, the fort showcases a blend of Hindu and Mughal architectural styles. The fort complex includes courtyards, temples, halls, and beautifully decorated palaces.

Architecture & Significance

Major attractions inside the fort include Sheesh Mahal, Diwan-e-Aam, Diwan-e-Khas, and Sukh Niwas. The intricate mirror work and carvings demonstrate the artistic excellence of ancient craftsmen.
