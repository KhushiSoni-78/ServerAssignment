Overview

Hawa Mahal, also known as the Palace of Winds, is one of the most recognizable structures of Jaipur. Its honeycomb-shaped façade makes it a unique architectural marvel.

History

Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, Hawa Mahal was designed to allow royal women to observe street festivals while remaining unseen.

Design & Importance

The palace has 953 small windows known as jharokhas, which allow cool air to circulate throughout the building. This natural ventilation system keeps the palace cool even during hot summers.
